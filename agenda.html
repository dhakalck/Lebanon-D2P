Unit 2: Economic Evaluation of Policy | June 15–18, 2026

Overview

Important Notes

  • Pre-Work: Review Unit 1 deliverables (problem statement, root causes, policy options) before Day 1
  • Required Materials: Bring laptops with Microsoft Excel installed
  • Templates: Decision tree Excel templates will be provided
  • Mentorship: Each group will have dedicated mentor time during group work sessions
  • Presentations: Use provided PowerPoint template for final presentations
  • Post-Training: Mentorship support continues through September for economic evaluation refinement

Unit 2 Learning Objectives

By the end of this 4-day training, participants will be able to:

L09: Framing Economic Evaluation

  • Understand key concepts in health economic evaluation
  • Design an evaluation plan applying perspective, time frame, and analytic horizon

L10: Assessing Health Impact

  • Understand why models are used in public health
  • Construct and populate decision tree models
  • Calculate expected values for health outcomes

L11: Cost Analysis

  • Develop comprehensive cost inventories
  • Identify and categorize different types of costs
  • Calculate intervention costs using decision trees

L12: Economic Evaluation

  • Construct full economic evaluation models
  • Calculate and interpret ICERs
  • Compare interventions using multiple evaluation techniques

L13: Sensitivity Analysis

  • Recognize the value of sensitivity analysis
  • Test model inputs and assumptions
  • Present uncertainty in economic evaluations
