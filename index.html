Data to Policy (D2P) Training
Unit 2: Economic Evaluation of Policy

Lebanon Training Program

June 15-18, 2026 | W Amman Hotel, Jordan

Building capacity for evidence-based health policy through economic evaluation

Welcome to D2P Unit 2 Training

This intensive 4-day training program is designed to equip Lebanese Ministry of Health staff and health policy professionals with advanced skills in economic evaluation of health policy options. As part of the Bloomberg Philanthropies Data for Health Initiative, this training builds upon the foundations established in Unit 1 and prepares participants to conduct rigorous cost-effectiveness analyses for their policy briefs.

Training Objectives

By the end of Unit 2, participants will be able to:

  • Frame an economic evaluation for health policy decisions
  • Construct decision tree models to assess health impacts
  • Develop comprehensive cost inventories for policy interventions
  • Calculate and interpret cost-effectiveness ratios
  • Perform sensitivity analysis to account for uncertainty
  • Apply economic evaluation findings to policy recommendations

Policy Brief Topics

Two teams are developing policy briefs on critical health priorities:

Policy Brief 1

Strengthening Health Technology Assessment (HTA) to Address Rising Oncology Costs in Lebanon

Focus: Implementing institutionalized HTA to ensure cost-effective oncology treatment decisions

Policy Brief 2

Priority-Setting Mechanism for Health Technology Assessment (HTA) in Lebanon

Focus: Establishing systematic priority-setting frameworks for HTA implementation

Participants

This training brings together 12 participants from Lebanon, organized into two teams:

  • Group 1: 6 participants focused on HTA for Oncology Costs
  • Group 2: 6 participants focused on Priority-Setting Mechanisms

Each group includes members with expertise in:

  • Health policy development
  • Health economics and evaluation
  • Epidemiology and data analysis
  • Health program planning and operations

Expected Deliverables

During Unit 2, each team will develop:

  • Decision tree models for policy options
  • Comprehensive cost inventories
  • Health impact assessments
  • Cost-effectiveness analyses with sensitivity testing
  • Economic evaluation sections for policy briefs
  • Presentation of preliminary findings
